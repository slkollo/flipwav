Best Audio Format for Podcasting in 2026

Podcasting Audio Guide April 2026 · 6 min read

Starting a podcast and confused about audio formats? Should you use MP3, WAV, or AAC? The short answer is MP3 — but the full picture is more nuanced. Here's everything you need to know to get your podcast sounding great on every platform.

Quick Answer

Use MP3 at 128kbps (mono) or 192kbps (stereo) for distributing your podcast. Record and edit in WAV, then convert to MP3 for your final export.

Why MP3 Wins for Podcasting

MP3 has been the podcast standard since the very beginning — and for good reason. Every podcast platform, app, and device on the planet supports it. Spotify, Apple Podcasts, Google Podcasts, Overcast — all of them handle MP3 perfectly.

WAV files are 10x larger than MP3 with essentially no audible difference for spoken word audio. For music, the difference matters more. For a podcast? Your listeners won't notice.

The Ideal Podcast Workflow

01

Record in WAV

Always record your raw audio as WAV (44.1kHz, 16-bit). This preserves everything for editing.

02

Edit in WAV

Edit, cut, add music, and mix in your DAW. Keep everything as WAV to avoid quality loss.

03

Export as MP3

Final export: MP3, 128kbps mono (speech only) or 192kbps stereo (music + speech).

04

Upload to Platform

Upload your MP3 to Spotify, Apple Podcasts, Buzzsprout, or any hosting platform.

Mono or Stereo?

For most podcasts — especially interview or solo shows — mono is better. Here's why:

Mono cuts your file size in half. And since most people listen on headphones or small speakers, stereo gives no real benefit for speech audio. Save stereo for music-heavy podcasts or audio dramas where positioning matters.

Recommended settings: MP3 · 128kbps · Mono · 44.1kHz — for a standard talk or interview podcast. This gives excellent quality at around 1MB per minute.

What Bitrate Should I Use?

For speech-only podcasts, 128kbps mono is the sweet spot. It's the industry standard used by most major podcasts. Going higher wastes bandwidth without noticeable quality improvement for spoken word.

If your podcast includes music or sound effects, consider 192kbps stereo for a better listening experience.
